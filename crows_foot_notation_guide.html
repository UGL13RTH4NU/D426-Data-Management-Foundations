Crow's Foot Notation

D426 Data Management Foundations — Visual Reference Guide

What Is Crow's Foot Notation?

Crow's foot notation is a way to show cardinality (maximum) and modality (minimum) of relationships in an ER diagram. It uses three simple symbols placed at each end of a relationship line.

Key OA rule: The outer symbol (farthest from the entity box) = minimum (modality). The inner symbol (closest to the entity box) = maximum (cardinality).

The Three Base Symbols

Zero

○ Circle

Means "zero" — the relationship is optional. Used as an inner symbol (minimum).
One

| Short Line (Bar)

Means "one" — exactly one instance. Can be outer (max=1) or inner (min=1, mandatory).
Many

⋔ Crow's Foot (Fork)

Means "many" — one or more (no upper limit). Used as an outer symbol (max=many). This is the namesake!

The Four Combinations You Must Know

Each end of a relationship line gets a pair: outer symbol (max) + inner symbol (min). There are four possible pairs:

Exactly One (One and only one)

|| — bar + bar — min: 1, max: 1
The entity must participate (min=1) and can relate to at most one instance (max=1). Mandatory, single.
Example: "A salesperson works in exactly one office."

Zero or One (Optional single)

|○ — bar + circle — min: 0, max: 1
The entity may or may not participate (min=0), but if it does, at most one instance (max=1). Optional, single.
Example: "An office may be occupied by zero or one salesperson."

One or Many (Mandatory many)

⋔| — crow's foot + bar — min: 1, max: many
The entity must participate (min=1) and can relate to many instances (max=many). Mandatory, multiple.
Example: "A department must have at least one employee (and can have many)."

Zero or Many (Optional many)

⋔○ — crow's foot + circle — min: 0, max: many
The entity may or may not participate (min=0), and if it does, can relate to many instances (max=many). Optional, multiple.
Example: "A salesperson may have zero customers, or many customers."

How to Read a Crow's Foot Diagram

Always read from one entity toward the other. The symbols you encounter at the far end (next to the other entity) tell you how many of that entity relate to the one you started from.

→ Reading left to right: A salesperson sells to zero or many customers (look at the symbols next to CUSTOMER).

← Reading right to left: A customer buys from exactly one salesperson (look at the symbols next to SALESPERSON).

This makes it a one-to-many (1:M) relationship.

The Three Relationship Types

One-to-One (1:1)

→ An employee works in exactly one office.
← An office is occupied by zero or one employee.

One-to-Many (1:M)

→ A department has one or many employees.
← An employee belongs to exactly one department.

Many-to-Many (M:M)

→ A student enrolls in zero or many courses.
← A course is taken by zero or many students.
